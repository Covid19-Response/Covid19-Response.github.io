    Pulse Oximetry

    • Overview

    We are a team of NUS researchers working to use existing technology and data analytical methods to respond to the Covid-19 crisis in foreign worker domitories.

    Personal Oximeter

    Measures the amount of oxygen in your blood

    Wireless Nodes

    Records patients' readings and uploads to cloud

    Measurements

    Receive notifications about patients' readings from the cloud

    Pulse Oximeter Usage

    navigate_before navigate_next

    Installation of Wireless Node

    Attach the wireless node to the wall and plug in to an outlet

    DO NOT TAMPER WITH NODE OR OUTLET

    Clip on Pulse Oximeter

    Clip on the index finger within 6m of any wireless node

    Stay still during measurement and wait 30 seconds for the measurements to finish

    The LED will turn blue to show that it is connected, green to show data, red when the reading ends

    Then take off the pulse oximeter

    Use your own device

    Up to 8 pulse oximeters can be connected at the same time

    SMS Alerts

    Both the dorm operators and the residents will receive SMS alerts if the measurements are abnormal

    1. The resident is asked to retake measurement in 1 hour in preferred language

    2. The dorm operator is alerted if the readings are abnormal again OR if patient does not retake measurement

    3. Resident receives an "all clear" if the readings are normal

    Dashboard

    Shows the latest measurements from each resident

    Access dashboard by computer, tablet or smartphone

    The colors show recency and normality of the measurements

    Grey: Measurement is not recent

    Green: Measurement is recent and normal

    Orange: One of the measurements is abnormal

    Red: Both measurements are abnormal

    The colors reset to grey at 8am, 12pm, 4pm and 8pm every day

    More Details

    Dashboard

    How to use?

    Clicking on the box shows the patient’s last 3 days of measurement

    Click on “Show all days” to see all the measurements taken from that patient.
